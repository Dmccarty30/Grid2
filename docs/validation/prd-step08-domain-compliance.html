Detailed Section Assessments

1. Grid Compliance (grid_compliance)

Not Applicable

BMAD Requirement: NERC standards, grid compliance, SCADA systems integration.

Assessment

Grid2 is a damage assessment and contractor management application. It does not:

  • Connect to, control, or monitor any Bulk Electric System (BES) cyber assets.
  • Interact with SCADA, EMS, or any operational technology systems.
  • Transmit control signals or receive real-time telemetry from grid infrastructure.
  • Process or store Critical Energy Infrastructure Information (CEII) as defined by FERC.
PRD Evidence (Executive Summary): "Grid2 is not a generic field service app. It is built for the specific operational reality of utility damage assessment contracting -- chaotic ticket intake, seasonal workforce activation, hazardous field conditions, and strict utility client data requirements."

NERC CIP standards (CIP-002 through CIP-014) apply to entities that own, operate, or maintain BES cyber systems. Grid2 is used by a contracting company that assesses damage for utilities; it is not a BES cyber asset, nor does it have electronic access to any BES cyber system. The domain signal "energy" is a broad classifier, and the grid_compliance section is designed for applications that directly interface with grid infrastructure.

Justification for N/A Rating

  • Grid2 is a business operations tool (workforce management + field data collection), not a grid operations tool.
  • Utility ticket data (circuit IDs, addresses) is operational context, not CEII or BES cyber asset data.
  • The PRD correctly addresses data security for sensitive utility information under NFR10 without over-claiming grid compliance.
Recommendation: No action required. However, consider adding a single sentence to the Domain-Specific Requirements section explicitly noting: "Grid2 does not interface with grid control systems and is not subject to NERC CIP standards." This preempts the question during architecture review.

2. Safety Protocols (safety_protocols)

Partial

BMAD Requirement: Safety requirements, safety protocols for workers in hazardous environments.

What the PRD Covers

PRD Evidence -- Safety & Compliance Documentation (Domain-Specific Requirements):
  • Job Safety Analyses (JSAs) integrated into the app -- daily safety briefing documentation per team/crew.
  • Accident Reports -- standardized form, triggerable from any active storm session.
  • Incident Reports -- separate form for near-misses and safety concerns.
  • Safety forms are "emergency-context UX" -- always accessible ("panic button"), minimal taps, auto-capture of GPS/timestamp/user context.
  • All safety documentation tied to project, date, crew, and location for audit trail.
PRD Evidence -- Field Usability:
  • Touch targets: 48x48pt minimum for primary field actions, designed for gloved operation in wet/stressful conditions.
  • PPE Acknowledgment step in onboarding wizard.
  • "Field Mode" toggle for maximum contrast outdoor conditions.

Gaps Identified

Gap 2.1 -- MVP Safety Gap: JSA, incident, and accident forms are explicitly deferred to Phase 2 (see "Deliberately Cut from MVP" table: "JSA / Incident / Accident forms -- Use paper for safety forms in first storm"). The PRD does not define what the MVP fallback process looks like. Workers will be in hazardous environments (downed power lines, storm debris) during Phase 1 with no in-app safety documentation capability.
Gap 2.2 -- No Safety SOP Reference: While the PRD mentions "SOPs" and "code of conduct" in the onboarding journey, there is no reference to the specific safety protocols that govern work around downed power lines, energized equipment, or hazardous weather conditions. The domain inherently involves life-safety risks.
Gap 2.3 -- No Emergency Contact / Escalation for Safety Incidents: The environmental hazard section defines an escalation chain (Contractor -> Team Lead -> Admin -> Super Admin -> Utility). No equivalent escalation chain is defined for worker safety incidents (injury, near-miss, medical emergency).
Gap 2.4 -- No Downed Line / Energized Equipment Protocol: The PRD mentions "hazardous field conditions" in the executive summary but does not specify how the app handles the most common field hazard: encountering downed or potentially energized lines during assessment.

Recommendations

R2.1: Add a "Phase 1 Safety Fallback" subsection to Domain-Specific Requirements. Document that paper-based JSA/incident/accident forms will be used during MVP, and specify that the Super Admin is responsible for distributing and collecting paper safety forms. This makes the gap intentional and documented rather than an oversight.
R2.2: Add a reference to the company's existing safety SOP documentation. Even if the SOP lives outside Grid2 in Phase 1, the PRD should acknowledge its existence and note that Phase 2 will digitize it.
R2.3: Define a worker safety incident escalation chain parallel to the environmental hazard chain. Include emergency services (911) as the first step for injuries.
R2.4: Consider adding a minimal "Emergency / Safety Alert" feature to the MVP -- even if it is just a prominent button that calls a pre-configured emergency phone number and auto-sends GPS coordinates via SMS. This is a low-complexity, high-value safety feature.

3. Environmental Compliance (environmental_compliance)

Partial

BMAD Requirement: Environmental regulations, hazard reporting, contamination protocols.

What the PRD Covers

PRD Evidence -- Environmental Hazard Reporting (Domain-Specific Requirements):
  • Immediate notification chain when environmental hazard identified (leaking transformer, PCB exposure).
  • Structured form enforced -- dropdown for hazard type, required photo, auto-captured GPS + timestamp.
  • Escalation path: Contractor -> Team Lead/Admin -> Super Admin -> Utility (per their protocol).
  • Environmental reports flagged separately from standard damage assessments.
  • Priority sync channel -- environmental reports jump the offline sync queue.
PRD Evidence -- Data Retention: "Environmental hazard reports flagged for extended retention -- contamination claims can surface years later."

Gaps Identified

Gap 3.1 -- Phase 1 Deferral: Environmental hazard alerts are explicitly cut from MVP ("Environmental hazard alerts -- Call utility directly (existing process). Returns in Phase 2"). The PRD does not document what the existing manual process looks like or what responsibilities the app leaves to the user during Phase 1.
Gap 3.2 -- No Regulatory Citations: The PRD does not reference specific environmental regulations that govern hazardous material reporting in the utility sector (e.g., EPA TSCA regulations for PCBs, CERCLA/Superfund notification requirements, state environmental agency reporting obligations). These regulations may impose mandatory reporting timelines (e.g., immediate notification to the National Response Center for PCB spills above certain thresholds).
Gap 3.3 -- No Hazard Classification Taxonomy: The PRD mentions "dropdown for hazard type" but does not define the taxonomy. For environmental compliance, the classification system matters because different hazard types trigger different regulatory reporting requirements and timelines.
Gap 3.4 -- Utility Notification Protocol Undefined: The escalation path ends with "Utility notified per their protocol" but does not address what happens if the utility protocol is unknown or if the environmental hazard exceeds what the utility protocol covers (e.g., a spill requiring EPA notification independent of the utility).

Recommendations

R3.1: Document the Phase 1 manual environmental hazard reporting process. Specify: who calls the utility, what information must be communicated, and whether Grid2 captures any record of the event (even a ticket note) during MVP.
R3.2: Add a brief regulatory reference section noting applicable environmental regulations (EPA TSCA for PCBs, state-level reporting requirements). This does not need to be exhaustive but should demonstrate awareness of the regulatory landscape and inform Phase 2 form design.
R3.3: Define a preliminary hazard classification taxonomy for Phase 2 planning: (1) PCB/chemical contamination, (2) oil/fluid spill, (3) vegetation/wildlife impact, (4) water body contamination, (5) soil contamination. This taxonomy drives the structured form design.
R3.4: Clarify the boundary of Grid Electric's environmental reporting responsibility vs. the utility client's responsibility. This is a business process question that impacts Phase 2 feature design.
