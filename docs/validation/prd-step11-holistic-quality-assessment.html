#1 — Highest Impact
Document and Justify Product Brief Divergences
Add a section (or appendix) titled "Scope Decisions — Departures from Product Brief" that explicitly documents the three significant changes:
  1. Auth: Entra ID SSO → Supabase email/password. State the reason (e.g., 30-day timeline, Entra ID complexity, deferred to Phase 2). Document the migration path back to SSO.

  3. PWA scaffold/service worker deferred. State the reason (online-only simplification). Document Phase 2 reintroduction.
Also acknowledge the positive divergences (assessment form and ticket management pulled forward) with rationale.
Impact: Eliminates the risk of a stakeholder (or future developer) discovering silent scope changes and losing trust in the document. Ensures architectural decisions are traceable.

#3 — Medium-High Impact
Resolve or Time-Bound the Open TODO
The TODO at lines 102 and 198 ("Detailed SOP Required — David will provide the comprehensive end-to-end SOP document from work") has no deadline or fallback strategy. Given the 30-day MVP timeline, this SOP is a blocking dependency for the Super Admin workflow design.

Recommended actions:

  • Define a fallback: "If SOP is not provided by [date], proceed with the generalized journey framework as documented. Refine post-launch based on operational feedback."
  • Identify the specific FRs that may change based on SOP input (likely FR11–FR14 and FR15–FR18) so the team knows which implementations are at risk of rework.
Impact: Converts an open-ended dependency into a bounded risk with a mitigation strategy. Prevents the TODO from silently blocking progress or being forgotten.
